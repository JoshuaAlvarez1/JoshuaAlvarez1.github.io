The past few years have been high-yielding milestones for the development of A.I. The rate of intelligence is accelerating and growing by the millisecond. The power that artificial intelligence yields is controversial in society.
The term, Artificial Intelligence was first coined in 1956 at a conference in Dartmoth. Since then, it's progressed from a checkers program, to drones that use biometrics to detect and kill targets, to a docile humanoid robot. It's been a productive development process that the globe has found useful.
A.I may worry the general public in regards to privacy and security. Our phones use biometrics like Face ID, fingerprint scanning, voice commands, and data analytics. By now, your database knows more about you than you know about yourself. Elon Musk himself, warns about the scary outcomes of A.I sentience overdeveloping. He even wants to counter this potential outcome with his own humanoid robot prototype, the Tesla Bot.
A.I has is beneficial in plenty of ways. It's simplified our way of life, and accelerated the development of technology. Although we love it, our dependence on A.I makes us vulnerable to its opposition. In conclusion, The better A.I gets, the weaker our security gets.
